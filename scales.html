I picked up the shell of soap, it was meant to be a scallop, although the ridges had now mostly disappeared, I could see my hand through the amber glycerin. I rubbed the stone into my hands and let it soften in the water, then squeezed, leaving the shards like leaves of glass in the bottom of the sink.

I could hear him putting his cup down in the other room, the soft, hollow knock of ceramic on metal, I looked into the mirror and waited, the sweat gathering in my armpits and sliding down the ridge of my back, the hair above my neck slick with it, all was quiet in the living room.
